Repuck Hockey

Broken sticks, too many pucks wasted — and a better way to play on the street.

Product & opportunity

What we sell — and why it can work

Repuck Hockey is a consumer and team-focused product line: street hockey pucks made by grinding broken composite sticks, blending the fiber with recycled plastic, and injection-molding a puck tuned for driveway and rink-adjacent concrete. We also sell the story — verifiable recycled content and local collection partnerships.

We see a viable market because (1) hockey households already buy pucks and balls repeatedly, (2) sustainability narratives move product in youth sports, and (3) rinks and associations need a constructive answer to “what do we do with snapped sticks?” beyond the dumpster.

Mission

Why we exist

Repuck Hockey keeps broken composite sticks out of landfills by turning them into durable, affordable street hockey pucks — so more kids can keep playing and families waste less money on gear that still has life left in the material.

The problem

Hockey is a very expensive sport, and that cost keeps many families on the sidelines. Gear adds up fast — and one of the biggest pain points is equipment that fails before its time.

$350–400Typical high-end stick price
Too oftenSticks break anyway

Waste worth fixing

Expensive sticks break more than they should for what families pay. When they snap, the usual answer is the trash — but that carbon fiber and composite material still has life in it.

Repuck asks: what if those broken sticks were recycled and reused instead of thrown away?

How it works

Partner with construction and recycling groups to collect broken sticks, process them safely, and give the material a second life.

Step 1 Collect broken sticks
Step 2 Grind into usable fiber / particles
Step 3 Blend with plastic
Step 4 Mold into street hockey pucks

The mix is tuned so pucks slide smoothly on concrete — built for driveway and pavement games, not the landfill.

Disruption

Are we “disruptive”?

We are not trying to replace elite on-ice puck suppliers overnight. We are disruptive to the default story of street hockey gear: cheap virgin plastic, zero provenance, no link to the hockey ecosystem’s waste stream.

  • Supply-side twist: feedstock from broken sticks lowers disposal pain for rinks and creates a differentiated raw-material narrative.
  • Category expansion: “circular” performance pucks sit between commodity street pucks and premium training aids on shelf talkers and DTC landing pages.
  • Risk: incumbents can copy the idea if recycling at scale is proven — first-mover advantage is collection network + brand trust, not the mold alone.

Go-to-market

How we acquire customers

  • DTC Shopify — starter 2-packs and 3-packs; geo-targeted ads in hockey-dense ZIP codes.
  • Rink & association pilots — co-branded collection bins; coupon on-pack for first online repurchase.
  • Tournament & tryout season — booth + “trade your busted stick” for a discount code.
  • Creator / coach micro-influencers — paid product seeding (~25 creators in launch states).
  • Amazon storefront — after 90 days of QC data (reviews matter for commodity adjacency).

Retention

How we keep customers

  • Subscribe & save light — “season pack” every 4 months for driveway programs (optional skip).
  • League loyalty pricing — volume tiers for coaches who reorder each season.
  • Take-back — return worn Repucks (mailer label) for 15% off next order; material goes back into lower-spec batches.
  • Email drills + local events — not spam: one useful drill video per month during outdoor season.
